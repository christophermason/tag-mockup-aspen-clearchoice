## D · Generation Notes

### Style guardrails (apply to every generation):
- **Skin should look like skin.** Never airbrushed. Pores, freckles, fine lines visible at portrait scale.
- **Lighting is from one source.** Avoid the flat "Instagram fill" look. Real shadows matter.
- **No staring at camera unless specified.** Subjects looking past, away, or down read documentary, not advertising.
- **No "before/after" framing.** ClearChoice in particular — never imply transformation through dental-marketing photo conventions.
- **Aspirational but not impossible.** Subjects are 95th-percentile attractive of their age group, not models.
- **No brand watermarks, no logos.** The product overlay carries that.

### Sora vs. Imagen vs. Midjourney
- **Best for portraits (B1, B2, B3, B5):** Midjourney v6+ with `--style raw --ar 4:3 --stylize 50`
- **Best for hero composition (C1):** Sora — handles real product devices better
- **Best for still life (A3 small cards, C1):** Imagen 3 or Adobe Firefly
- **Avoid DALL·E 3 for faces** — soft, plasticky, gives away the comp.

### Integration once images exist
Each gradient block in `index.html` has a class like `.feed-hero` or `.ipd-img`. Drop the corresponding image into `/photos/` and update CSS:
```css
.feed-hero {
  background:
    linear-gradient(180deg, transparent 50%, rgba(15,27,46,0.78) 100%),
    url('photos/A2_drpatel.jpg') center/cover;
}
```
The dark gradient overlay at the bottom is essential — never put text on raw photography without it.
